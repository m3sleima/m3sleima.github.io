Real Deal is a concept app that helps users find the cheapest groceries. It's a shopper's personal on-the-go price checker.

This is a personal project that involves the use of The Engineering Design Process to create a final prototype of the mobile app. The process included things such as: problem definition, situation of concern, design specification, concept generation and evaluation, branding and prototyping (wireframes, visual mock-ups), all completed in 10 weeks.

Problem Definition: Students are spending too much money on groceries because they don't have time to look for the cheapest options.

Solution: Real Deal saves students both time and money by locating the cheapest groceries around town.



SITUATION OF CONCERN

Real Deal was designed to minimize the amount of money and effort spent on purchasing groceries. The app prompts the user to enter his/her desired grocery list and finds the best promotions around town. The app then compiles a list of the items and where the cheapest options are found so the user can easily price match and purchase all the items at one location. The primary users of this product are people with a relatively low budget and a tight schedule, a perfect example: students. Generally speaking, these individuals are fairly knowledgeable with using mobile applications and would do anything to save a buck or two. This is their "quick and dirty" way to save money.

OPTIONS

There are 4 different options that you can choose from: Browse Flyers, Create List, Resume List and View History

BROWSE FLYERS: Simply browse through the various stores' weekly flyers. Once you find something you like, click the add button located in the top right corner of the screen.

CREATE LIST: Create a list of items you already have in mind.


Once you create your list, select an option: Find Cheapest or Select Brands

Algorithm runs and figures out which items are/are not available in the weekly flyers.
Red - unavailable items
Black - available items

Choose to:
"Find Cheapest!" - takes you to your compiled Grocery List
or
"Select Brands" - allows you to individually select your items' brands (in case you have a preference).


Use your list and SAVE, SAVE, SAVE!
